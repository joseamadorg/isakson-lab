Meet the Team

Brant Isakson

PhD | Principal Investigator (PI)

Leads the Isakson Lab at UVA’s CVRC, studying endothelial–smooth muscle communication to advance therapies for hypertension, inflammation, and cardiometabolic disease.

Angela Best

Lab Manager

Manages daily operations, compliance, and training; coordinates research projects and supports experiments to keep workflows efficient and organized.

Luke Dunaway

PhD | Research Assistant Professor

I study iron regulation of endothelial biology in the conditions such as iron deficiency anemia and chronic kidney disease.

Brooke O'Donnell

PhD | Post-Doctoral Researcher

Focuses on understanding how PANX1 post-translational modifications, specifically phosphorylation of Y198, S205 and Y308, can affect vascular changes at the level of the organism and the mechanisms responsible.

Madison D. Williams

PhD | Post-Doctoral Researcher

I am a postdoctoral fellow in the Isakson Lab. I study pannexin channels and how they control renin release from juxtaglomerular cells and therefore blood pressure.

Skylar Loeb

PhD Candidate

Examines purinergic and iron-related signaling in small vessels and how these pathways influence vascular tone and organ function.

Wyatt Schug

PhD Candidate

I study how oxidative stress in cardiometabolic disease drives basophil activation and immune cell programming.

Zuzanna Juśkiewicz

PhD Candidate

Works in the study of lymphatic endothelial cell PIEZO2 channel function in context of lymphangiogenesis and obesity development.

Alicia Carvalho

Undergraduate Researcher

Assists with imaging, data analysis, and molecular assays that support ongoing research on vascular communication and signaling pathways.

Nasim Abib

Undergraduate Researcher

Supports experiments on microvascular signaling and helps maintain laboratory data, sample organization, and experimental documentation.

Clay Grisius

Undergraduate Researcher

Works with Skylar on her project characterizing the role of lymphatic endothelial alpha globin in mice with cardiometabolic heart failure.

Brianna Kalina

Undergraduate Researcher

I work with Zuza on her project studying lymphatic endothelial cell PIEZO2 channel function in context of lymphangiogenesis and obesity development.

Samantha Bielefeld

Undergraduate Researcher

I work with Brooke on characterizing post-translational modifications of Y308, Y198 and S205 on PANX1 and how they impact changes in the vasculature.

Taylor Buckley

Undergraduate Researcher

I work with Madison to explore the role of pannexins in renal hemodynamics and renin recruitment.

Nirelle Sitchoa

Undergraduate Researcher

Supports data curation, documentation, and analysis, assisting the team with testing hypotheses about vascular communication mechanisms.
