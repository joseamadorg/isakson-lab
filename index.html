What has the Isakson's Lab been working on recently?

All of our projects center on cellular communication within the microcirculation, spanning topics from lymphatic function in fat and heart failure, to the interactions between inflammatory cells and the vascular wall, and the roles of iron and ATP in the circulation and kidneys

What are the most promising clinical implications of the lab's research?

There are several promising clinical applications in the field of cardiovascular disease. While much of our research directly relates to blood pressure regulation and conditions such as hypertension, our newer work extends into cardiometabolic disease, encompassing mechanisms that link obesity to heart failure.

Isakson Lab research is focused on:

Endothelial‚ÄìSmooth Muscle Signaling

Defining how adjacent vascular cells communicate to control tone and blood pressure.

Microcirculation in Disease

Linking small-vessel dysfunction to hypertension, stroke risk, and tissue injury.

Lymphatic Function

Studying lymphatics in adipose tissue and heart failure to understand fluid and immune balance.

Inflammation & Vessel Wall

Defining how inflammatory cells interact with endothelium to drive vascular injury.

Iron & ATP Signaling

Probing how iron and extracellular ATP regulate vascular function in the circulation and kidney.

Bench-to-Bedside Translation

From molecular and mouse models to IRB-approved human studies aimed at new therapies.
